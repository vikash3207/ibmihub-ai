# Where to Go Next

## Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will have a clear picture of what you have
covered across the IBM i Fundamentals path, understand that you now have a
mental map rather than full mastery, and know some practical directions for
continuing to learn.

## Simple Explanation

Across this path, you have built up a picture of IBM i as an integrated
platform, why it continues to matter to real businesses, and the major pieces
that make it up: libraries and objects, the 5250 interface, physical and
logical files, RPGLE, CLLE, Db2 for i, job logs and spool files, and a general
sense of how development work flows from source code to a released program.

It is worth being honest about what this means and does not mean. You now have
a mental map of IBM i: a set of concepts and how they relate to each other.
You do not yet have full mastery of any one of these topics. That is expected,
and it is a completely normal place to be after an introductory path. Real
comfort with IBM i comes from continued, gradual exposure: reading existing
programs, asking questions, and connecting new details back to the concepts
you have already built here.

IBM i Fundamentals is the foundation of a larger IBM i curriculum IBMiHub AI
is building out over time -- deeper coverage of RPGLE and CLLE, Db2 for i and
SQL, interactive screens and subfiles, and troubleshooting in production
settings, among other topics. None of that deeper content exists yet; this
path is where it starts.

## Why It Matters

Setting realistic expectations matters. If you expected to be an expert after
one introductory path, you might feel discouraged when you encounter something
unfamiliar. Recognizing that this path gave you a foundation, not the finish
line, makes it easier to keep learning steadily rather than feeling like you
have fallen behind.

## Practical Example

Imagine a new developer joining a team that maintains IBM i business
applications. In their first weeks, they spend time reading existing programs
to see how earlier lessons' concepts show up in practice, watch how more
experienced colleagues check job logs when troubleshooting an issue, and
gradually take on small pieces of RPGLE or CLLE work under guidance.

Over time, and without trying to learn everything at once, they become
comfortable navigating the platform. This is a simplified, illustrative
example rather than a real system, but it reflects a common, realistic pattern
for how comfort with IBM i tends to develop.

## Common Confusions

**"Do I understand everything about IBM i now that I have finished this
path?"**
No. This path gave you a foundational mental map, not full mastery. Real
comfort with any of these topics comes from continued exposure and practice
over time.

**"Is there one correct order to learn everything next?"**
No. Several directions are reasonable depending on your goals, such as
practicing 5250 navigation, learning more RPGLE or CLLE basics, gradually
understanding Db2 for i and SQL, getting comfortable reading job logs and
spool files during troubleshooting, or learning from existing programs and
business processes. As IBMiHub AI adds more structured content over time,
you will be able to pick a specific direction -- such as RPGLE, CLLE, or
troubleshooting -- rather than guessing where to look next.

**"Are deeper topics like advanced RPGLE, troubleshooting, or
integrations already available right now?"**
No, not yet. Topics like these are planned as part of a larger curriculum
IBMiHub AI is actively building, not features that are already built or
promised on a specific timeline. Treat them as areas worth exploring once
they are published, not something you are missing today.
