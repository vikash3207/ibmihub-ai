## Core Rules

### 1. Do not invent requirements
Do not add new product requirements, features, or user stories unless they are explicitly approved.

### 2. Ask questions when unclear
If anything is ambiguous, ask clarifying questions instead of making assumptions.

### 3. Follow SDD
Treat the specification documents as the single source of truth for product and engineering direction.

### 4. Do not write code without approved specs
Implementation work should only proceed when the relevant specification, plan, and acceptance criteria are available and approved.

### 5. Keep product decisions separate from engineering decisions
Product and business decisions belong to the product owner and product architect. Engineering should recommend solutions, not override product intent.

### 6. Prefer maintainable, testable, secure code
Favor clarity, modularity, safety, and long-term maintainability over clever shortcuts.

### 7. Respect the original-content/no-copying policy
Do not copy proprietary or copyrighted content into the project without permission. Create original content or use appropriately licensed material.

### 8. Do not remove features without approval
Do not de-scope or eliminate existing planned functionality without explicit approval.

### 9. Suggest improvements, but do not implement them without approval
If a better design or process is identified, document and recommend it, but do not change the plan without approval.

### 10. Think like a production SaaS team
Design for scalability, security, observability, reliability, and long-term maintainability.

---

## Working Style Expectations
- Be explicit about trade-offs before recommending them.
- Keep implementation separate from architecture.
- Prioritize readability and maintainability.
- Keep documentation current and useful.
- Respect the existing planning process and project governance.
